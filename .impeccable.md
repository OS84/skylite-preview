# Skylite — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Two equal audiences:

**Professionals** (architects, interior designers, developers, contractors): They visit to specify products, validate technical credibility, and determine whether Skylite is a serious engineering partner worth naming in a project. They scan fast, want proof of expertise, and respond to precision — named projects, compliance standards, material specs.

**Private homeowners** (penthouse owners, villa renovations): They visit to be inspired, to imagine what Skylite could do for their space. They respond to beautiful photography, the emotional promise of light and sky entering a room, and trust signals like decades of experience and prestigious references.

Both audiences need to leave feeling: "these people are the best at what they do."

### Brand Personality
- **Engineering excellence** — precision, technical authority, zero-compromise manufacturing
- **Decades of experience** — 40 years, the Knesset dome, the National Library, מצפה הימים — not a startup, an institution
- **Beautiful designs** — the product is inherently architectural and photogenic; the site should feel as considered as the products

Three words: **precise · experienced · luminous**

### Primary Goal
Convert: generate contact and quote inquiries. Secondary: establish Skylite as the undisputed category leader in Israel so architects specify them by name.

The site must do two things simultaneously — wow visitors emotionally (photography, light, space) and build rational confidence (engineering specs, compliance, named projects, 40 years). Neither alone is enough.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light, architectural, photography-led.** The product is about letting sky in — the site should feel like standing in a well-designed space with natural light flooding through. Not cold, not corporate, not SaaS.

- **Theme**: Light mode. The product is about light and openness — a dark site would contradict the brand promise.
- **Palette**: Teal accent (#2B7A8C) against near-white backgrounds. Clean, considered, not clinical.
- **Typography**: Heebo (Hebrew RTL constraint). Use weight contrast aggressively — 900 for headlines, 300 for body — to create the hierarchy a single font family needs to carry.
- **Photography**: The hero of the site. UI chrome should get out of the way of the images.
- **Avoid**: Cold/corporate fintech feel, generic card grids, anything that looks like Israeli government websites, dark-mode SaaS aesthetics, over-animated UI that distracts from the photography.

### Design Principles
1. **Light is the product** — every design decision should reinforce the idea that Skylite sells the experience of sky and light entering a building, not just a glass panel.
2. **Photography leads, UI follows** — the interface should be a frame for the images, not compete with them.
3. **Earned authority** — trust comes from specifics: named projects, real years, compliance standards. Never generic claims.
4. **Both audiences in one breath** — the emotional (homeowner) and rational (professional) cases should coexist on every page, not in separate sections.
5. **Israeli precision** — the brand is deeply rooted in Israel. Named local landmarks are a strength, not a limitation.
