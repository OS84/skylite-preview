## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Custom-engineered skylights and architectural glazing for projects that demand geometry no one else will attempt.

**What it does:** Skylite designs, manufactures, and installs skylights — fixed, retractable, walk-on, smoke-vent, and custom structural glazing (domes, pyramids, vaults). Every project is engineered bespoke: from sketch to structural calculation, fabrication, installation, and post-install service. 100% manufactured in-house in Petach Tikva, Israel.

**Product category:** Architectural skylights / roof glazing / custom aluminum-glass systems. On the shelf next to: *גגון אור*, *חלון גג*, *כיפות תאורה*, *שחרור עשן*.

**Product type:** Custom-manufactured product + turnkey engineering service. Every sale is project-specific, quoted per spec.

**Business model:** B2B + B2C. Per-project quoting; no price list. Architects spec the product into a building; contractors/owners pay.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem customers face:** When a design calls for custom or moving glass in the roof, the default vendors can't build it — and the ones who can are either abroad (slow, expensive, no local service) or unproven (risky on a public project).

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Imported systems (German/Italian):** Long lead times, import bureaucracy, replacement parts are months away, no Hebrew-speaking service tech
- **Local aluminum fabricators:** Can do a box window, can't do a 12m pyramid or a retractable pool roof
- **Generic catalog skylights:** Fixed sizes, no custom geometry, no motorization options

**What it costs them:**
- **A leaking skylight kills architect reputation.** It's the single worst failure mode — destroys client trust, triggers insurance claims, ends referral chains.
- Rework delays handover, client withholds final payment, contractor's margin gets crushed
- Project schedule slips cascade into every downstream trade
- Homeowner watches their dream feature turn into a stain on the ceiling

**Emotional tension:**
- Architect: *"Will this vendor actually pull off what I drew, or am I about to deliver a compromise to my client?"* — and — *"If this leaks, my phone rings for the next five years."*
- Contractor: *"Who do I call at 2am if this thing leaks?"*
- Homeowner: *"Is this going to be the thing that breaks in 10 years?"*

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators (per user, April 2026): engineering depth.**

Specifically:
- **Geometry no one else will attempt** — domes, pyramids, vaults, walk-on, retractable. Not "we'll try" but "we've done this 100 times"
- **Right first time, on time, in full** — every project is unique, many are complex. We lead the engineering, solve the hard parts upstream, and deliver a system that works on day one and stays dry for decades. No leaks. No rework. No handover slip.
- **40-year track record on named institutional projects** — National Library (ספריה הלאומית), Mitzpe Hayamim (מצפה הימים), Recanati Winery (יקב רקנאטי), Bar-Ilan University **(confirm: Knesset dome — is this still accurate? .impeccable.md mentions it)**
- **End-to-end ownership** — engineering, structural calc, manufacturing, installation, service, all in-house
- **Made in Israel** — full local accountability, fast response, Hebrew-speaking team, understands Israeli codes and climate

**How we do it differently:**
We don't ship catalog products. Every project starts with a sketch consultation and ends with installation — with rigorous QC at every step between. The architect works directly with our engineering team, not through a dealer. Complexity is our baseline: we engineer around the problem upstream, so the build site doesn't pay the price later.

**Why that's better:**
Projects stop failing at the glass stage. The architect's vision survives into built form. The contractor has one accountable vendor. The owner gets a system designed for Israel's specific sun, wind, rain, and code environment.

**Why customers choose us:**
Because they've tried the alternatives — imported brands that took 8 months to deliver, local fabricators who couldn't execute the drawing — and they or their colleague have seen Skylite pull off what looked impossible.
